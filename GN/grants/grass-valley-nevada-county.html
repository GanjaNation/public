GN Grants — Nevada County (Grass Valley) Guide

Last updated: Nov 7, 2025 • Scope: California only • Audience: small cannabis farm (<3 employees) pursuing eco-friendly, tourist-oriented model

Heads-up: Direct capital grants to marijuana businesses are rare; most federal ag programs exclude marijuana (hemp differs). In CA, workable lanes are: local equity funds, energy/storage rebates, air-district equipment grants, restoration via sponsors, and tax credits. Zoning/licensing controls what “tourism” can legally include.

1) Local, Cannabis-Specific Funds (Nevada County / Nearby)

Nevada County — Good to Grow (Round 2: 2025)

County program (GO-Biz funded) that supports local cannabis businesses via small grants, technical assistance, and/or fee support. 2025 pot ~$440k+ with awards historically up to ~$5,000 (varies by round).

  • Program overview
  • 2025 round notice / FAQ (archived)
  • Admin partner: Sierra Business Council

Use: Working capital, compliance/permits, consulting, limited equipment, fee offsets. Unlikely for land purchases.

Nevada City — Cannabis Equity Grants (adjacent)

Direct grants/fee relief for eligible equity applicants; Nevada City is ~10 minutes from Grass Valley.

  • City Cannabis Program / Equity Grants
  • Nevada City website

2) Energy & Storage (Solar/Batteries)

SGIP — Self-Generation Incentive Program (CPUC)

Rebates for behind-the-meter battery storage; pair with solar. Cannabis facilities are typically not excluded. Grass Valley is in PG&E territory.

  • CPUC SGIP overview
  • PG&E SGIP (incl. Equity/Resiliency)

Use: Lower capex for battery-backed operations (processing, small cold room, “tasting”/education space, trailer power, PSPS resiliency).

3) Equipment & Site Work

Northern Sierra AQMD — FARMER Program

CARB-funded grants to replace/upgrade ag equipment (tractors, ag pumps, certain trucks). Nevada County is in the Northern Sierra AQMD.

  • District grants hub
  • CARB FARMER program

Status note: A recent notice indicated no awards expected in 2025—still worth monitoring.

CDFW — Cannabis Restoration Grant Program (CRGP)

Funds restoration/mitigation (erosion control, crossings, water conservation, habitat). Money flows via eligible sponsors (public agencies/tribes/nonprofits) and can fund work benefiting private cultivation sites.

  • Program home

Use: If your parcel needs grading/erosion/water work with ecological benefit, partner with a sponsor to pursue CRGP for those elements.

Compost & Soil (General Ag)

Zero Foodprint — Compost Connector can offset compost costs via restaurant fee pools (not cannabis-specific).

4) Taxes & Business Relief

High-Road Cannabis Tax Credit (HRCTC)

CA Franchise Tax Board credit = 25% of qualified “high-road employer” spend (wages, training, benefits), up to $250,000 / year through 2027. Reservation required.

  • FTB HRCTC page

5) Zoning & Licensing Snapshot

Grass Valley (City)

Historically limited to distribution and nursery permits; city caps/lotteries applied. On-site retail or consumption not permitted inside city limits.

  • City ordinance PDF
  • City website (search “Cannabis”)

Unincorporated Nevada County

County rules specify zones for commercial cannabis uses, event rules/setbacks, and currently no on-site consumption. Retail allowances are limited/conditional (often in commercial/industrial zones; “Nevada-County-grown only”).

  • County Cannabis Program

Tourism Implications

A “tourist farm where visitors buy product and stay in trailers” likely requires: siting outside Grass Valley city, fitting County zoning, focusing on nursery/education/tours with no on-site consumption. Sales may need to route via a licensed retail premise in an allowed zone or delivery.

7) Next 60–90 Days (Quick Plan)

  1. Pin parcel & jurisdiction (GV city vs unincorporated county) and verify zoning/license type fit.
  2. Sketch energy design (solar + storage) and pre-qualify SGIP with an approved installer.
  3. Line up a CRGP sponsor if the site needs restoration-type work (maps/photos help).
  4. Assemble compliance package (local permits, site plan, budget) so you’re ready when Good to Grow reopens.
  5. Check equity eligibility (Nevada City) and pre-gather documents.
  6. Reserve HRCTC once your hiring/payroll plan is defined.
